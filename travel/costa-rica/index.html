Pura Vida, I Do: How a Wedding Budget Became a 10-Day Costa Rican Adventure.

Ten days, twelve people, and vows exchanged beneath 700-year-old trees on a Costa Rican beach. Nurse sharks, a surprise mariachi band, and hot springs under a volcano came with it.

Read the Full Story Start Planning Yours

A bus quote broke us: $2,000 to move 25 people, twice.

We did everything right — a dedicated wedding email, twenty venues toured across Colorado, prices tracked down to the napkins. And the second the word “wedding” touched anything, the price tripled. The breaking point: a quote for a 25-person shuttle running two short routes — $2,000.

So we asked what actually mattered: chasing adventure while we're young, staying financially free, and a wedding day that felt romantic instead of like a fire drill. Ten days in Costa Rica, with our people, with a wedding stitched into the middle. Done.

Adventure over ambiance. A beach over a ballroom. One perfect day, not one stressful year.

Jungle lodge, Caribbean coast, volcano country.

Rental cars with pre-existing damage. Mountain highways that drop a lane mid-curve with zero warning. We still got twelve people to a beach in Punta Uva exactly on schedule for a 4pm ceremony.

Before we said “I do.”

Day 4: Snorkeling with a Nurse Shark

Someone spotted “a really big fish” and our guide swam straight at it to prove there was nothing to fear — a nurse shark, completely unbothered. The park hike topped it: bats camouflaged on tree bark none of us would've ever spotted alone, plus sloths, monkeys, and iguanas.

Days 1–2 — Jungle Lodge

4am flights, chaotic San José driving, then a private rainforest lodge all to ourselves.

Day 3 — Caribbean Coast

Black sand, turquoise water, and a candlelit Argentinian dinner off the main road.

Day 5 — Finding “the Spot”

A wild dolphin pod, a beach entirely to ourselves, and stumbling onto our ceremony site.

Day 6: a beach ceremony beneath 700-year-old trees.

Bridal makeup at 10:30, a first look in the villa backyard, portraits at Manzanillo National Park (sweating through formalwear in tropical heat is a real occupational hazard). Then the ceremony at Punta Uva: an aisle of centuries-old trees, wild monkeys overhead, and a full setup built from scratch on a public beach that was never anyone's to rent out as a venue.

The vows were the real thing — a little shaky, a lot of tears held back. Dinner at SOCA was interrupted by a full mariachi band that appeared with zero warning and played through our first dance. Toasts ran long, the guest sombrero got signed as a keepsake, and dessert showed up as a tres leches cake nobody ordered and everybody needed.

The trip kept going after “I do.”

Day 7: Baby Sloths and Happy Tears

Breakfast turned into happy-tears-in-public territory once the adrenaline wore off. A private tour of the Jaguar Rescue Center delivered toucans, agoutis, and a room of three sloth babies so small they didn't look real.

Days 8–10: Waterfalls, a Volcano, and the Long Way Home

Hot springs every night at Los Lagos, a Maui-green waterfall detour, and an Arenal viewpoint hike that cleared to a full volcano view the second we finished it. A troop of coatis begged for snacks before one last scenic drive back to San José and flights home — closing the ten days that started as a rebellion against a $30K quote.
